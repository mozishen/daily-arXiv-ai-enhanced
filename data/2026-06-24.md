Method: 综述SKA先导望远镜（pathfinders/precursors）的观测证据，将射电遗迹与激波关联；同时考察相关激波影响现象（射电凤凰、GReET）。提出利用SKA-Low/Mid的分阶段部署（AA0.5至AA4）结合X射线观测，通过单个目标的连续谱/偏振精细研究和宽场巡天的遗迹统计研究两条路径来推进理解。

Result: 明确了射电遗迹作为并合动力学、粒子加速和磁场演化示踪物的关键地位；指出了SKA时代通过连续谱和偏振观测、遗迹样本统计研究将取得的突破；识别了科学验证目标，为SKA时代的激波物理研究奠定基础。

Conclusion: SKA-Low/Mid结合X射线观测能够显著推进对星系团并合激波微观物理和宇宙演化的理解，射电遗迹的精细研究和样本统计研究是关键手段。

Abstract: Galaxy cluster mergers represent the most energetic phenomena in the Universe since the Big Bang releasing gravitational potential energy of $\sim 10^{63-64}$ erg, injecting turbulence and driving shocks through the intracluster medium (ICM). These merger shocks can accelerate cosmic ray electrons and compress magnetic fields, sometimes producing Mpc-scale synchrotron radio structures known as radio relics. Radio relics are powerful tracers of merger dynamics, particle acceleration, and magnetic field evolution, yet fundamental questions about the underlying physics and their time evolution remain unresolved. In this chapter, we review the current understanding of cluster merger shocks and their radio signatures, presenting the observational evidence from the SKA pathfinders and precursors, linking radio relics to shocks alongside outstanding theoretical challenges. We also consider related shock-influenced phenomena: radio phoenices from revived fossil AGN plasma and Gently Re-Energised Tails. Further, we outline the directions of investigation using the sensitivities of the SKA-Low and Mid complemented with X-ray observations that will allow us to make significant progress in understanding the cluster merger shocks. Detailed studies of individual targets in continuum and polarization and studies of populations of relics using wide surveys will both provide insights to the micro-physics and cosmic evolution of merger shocks. We present SKAO capabilities across staged deployments starting from AA0.5 to AA4 and identify science verification targets that will illuminate the physics of cluster merger shocks in the SKA era
